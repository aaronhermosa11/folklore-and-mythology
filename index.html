Filipino Deities

Filipino mythology is rich with gods and goddesses, each with unique powers and roles. Here are some of the most significant ones:

  • Bathala – The supreme god in Tagalog mythology, creator of the world and ruler of the heavens.
  • Mayari – The goddess of the moon, daughter of Bathala, and known for her beauty and strength.
  • Lakapati – The goddess of fertility and abundance, widely worshipped by Filipino farmers.
  • Apung Iru – A supreme deity in Kapampangan mythology, said to govern both the heavens and earth.

Mythical Creatures

Filipino folklore is filled with fascinating creatures, often both terrifying and enchanting. Some of the most popular include:

  • Aswang – A shape-shifting monster known for its ability to suck blood, often associated with witches and vampires.
  • Kapre – A large, tree-dwelling creature that smokes cigars and is known for its mischievous ways.
  • Manananggal – A creature with the ability to separate its upper body from its lower half, usually depicted as a woman who flies at night to prey on victims.

Famous Legends

Many Filipino myths and legends explain the origins of the islands, the people, and their customs. Here are a few famous ones:

  • The Legend of Maria Makiling – A famous story about a beautiful diwata (fairy) who lived in the mountains of Laguna. She is known to protect the people of the region.
  • The Legend of the Pineapple – A folktale explaining how the pineapple fruit got its appearance, stemming from a daughter's disobedience and a mother's curse.
  • The Legend of the Mango – A popular tale about the origin of the mango tree, which was believed to have been blessed by the gods.
  • The Epic of Biag ni Lam-ang – A well-known Ilocano epic about a heroic figure named Lam-ang who performed many feats and conquered great dangers.

About Filipino Folklore and Mythology

Filipino folklore and mythology come from various regions in the Philippines, and they play a vital role in the culture and traditions of the Filipino people. These stories, passed down through generations, offer insights into Filipino beliefs, moral values, and the relationship between humans and the supernatural.

From ancient deities and mythical creatures to epic legends, Filipino mythology is an essential part of the nation's identity and cultural heritage.
